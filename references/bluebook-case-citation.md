This is a locally authored operating summary for citation audits. It is not a copy of The Bluebook. Use the official Bluebook or applicable local court rules for final filing decisions.

## Case Citation Shape

Typical full case citation:

```text
Case Name v. Case Name, Volume Reporter FirstPage, Pincite (Court Year).
```

Examples for pattern recognition:

```text
Brown v. Bd. of Educ., 347 U.S. 483, 495 (1954).
United States v. Prince Line, Ltd., 189 F.2d 386, 388 (2d Cir. 1951).
Warren Publ'g Co. v. Spurlock, 645 F. Supp. 2d 402 (E.D. Pa. 2009).
```

Do not treat these examples as exhaustive. State public-domain formats, slip opinions, unpublished decisions, and local rules may change required details.

## Audit Checklist

- Case name: should be shortened to the first listed opposing parties in citation form; avoid `vs.`; use `v.`.
- Party abbreviations: apply common legal citation abbreviations where appropriate. Do not abbreviate `United States` as `U.S.` when it is a party name.
- Reporter segment: require volume, reporter abbreviation, and first page for ordinary reporter citations.
- Reporter normalization: flag common noncanonical forms such as `US`, `S.Ct.`, `F3d`, `F.Supp.2d`, and `L.Ed.2d`.
- Pincites: ordinary page pincites follow the first page after a comma. Star-page citations use `at *N`.
- Parenthetical: include the deciding court and year unless the reporter/court combination makes court information unnecessary. U.S. Supreme Court citations to `U.S.` usually need only the year.
- Parallel citations: generic Bluebook-style audits do not require parallel citations unless public-domain citation rules, state/local rules, or the user-selected court requires them.
- Parentheticals after court/year: order weight-of-authority, quoting/citing, then explanatory parentheticals when they are present.
- Short forms: `Id.` and other short-form citations require a clear immediately preceding authority or contextual referent; CourtListener citation lookup will not validate them.

## Common Findings

| Finding | Example | Suggested Direction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Wrong versus marker | `Brown vs. Board` | Use `v.` in case citations. |
| Reporter punctuation missing | `576 US 644` | Normalize to `576 U.S. 644`. |
| Federal reporter missing periods | `350 F3d 694` | Normalize to `350 F.3d 694`. |
| Missing comma before pincite | `347 U.S. 483 495` | Use `347 U.S. 483, 495`. |
| Ordinary reporter uses `at` | `347 U.S. 483 at 495` | Use `347 U.S. 483, 495`. |
| Missing court for federal appellate case | `189 F.2d 386 (1951)` | Include circuit and year if known. |
| Party United States abbreviated | `U.S. v. Smith` | Use `United States v. Smith` in case name. |

## Limits

Bluebook compliance sometimes depends on the target court, jurisdiction, document type, and local rules. If the user identifies a filing court or journal, check that authority before finalizing corrections.
